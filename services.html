Our Mentorship & Coaching pillar targets precisely this gap, providing structured, high-intensity development for individuals and teams who need to operate at a higher level of institutional competence.

Corporate & Government

Workforce Productivity

Workforce productivity is not simply a function of hours worked or tasks completed. It is an institutional capacity — shaped by systems, culture, measurement frameworks, and the quality of leadership that sets expectations and holds them.

Our capacity building programmes for corporate and government sectors address the full spectrum of productivity: from individual performance habits to team dynamics, to the systemic conditions that allow or prevent high performance at scale.

Programme Areas

  • Institutional productivity audits — diagnosing systemic underperformance
  • Leadership development for middle and senior management
  • Performance management system design and implementation support
  • Team effectiveness and inter-departmental coordination workshops
  • Government agency capacity building for policy execution roles

03
Advocacy & Policy

Digital Economy Advocacy

Africa's digital economy is growing — but the value generated by that growth is disproportionately captured outside the continent. Indigenous content, local platforms, and African intellectual production remain systematically undervalued relative to their actual economic and cultural contribution.

Our Digital Economy Advocacy pillar exists to change the terms of that conversation at the policy and regulatory level, championing Indigenous Content as both an economic category deserving formal protection and a strategic asset for national development.

Advocacy Focus Areas

  • Indigenous Content policy frameworks for digital platforms and broadcasting
  • Regulatory advocacy for local content quotas and investment requirements
  • Economic valuation methodologies for African digital production
  • Engagement with government ministries on digital sovereignty frameworks
  • Public education on the economic stakes of content ownership and distribution

Who This Is For

Regulators Content Creators Media Companies Government Ministries Civil Society

04
Policy & Regulatory Design

Regulatory Innovation

Regulation is typically understood as a constraint on economic activity. We argue — and demonstrate — that well-designed regulatory frameworks are among the most powerful tools available to governments for creating entirely new categories of industry, employment, and economic participation.

This is not a theoretical position. It is a design discipline: understanding how regulatory architecture, properly constructed, can do the work of economic stimulus without public expenditure, debt, or the distortions that come with conventional industrial policy.

Advisory Areas

  • Regulatory gap analysis — identifying where the absence of regulation destroys economic potential
  • New industry creation through licensing, standards-setting, and certification frameworks
  • Informal economy formalisation strategies using regulatory incentives
  • Policy design workshops for government agencies seeking to use regulation creatively
  • Non-tax revenue architecture — using regulatory fees and compliance structures as sustainable fiscal instruments

Who This Is For

Government Ministries Regulatory Agencies Development Banks International NGOs
