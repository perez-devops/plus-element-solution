Mission & Mandate

What We Are Here to Do

Plus Element Solutions exists to close the gap between policy formulation and policy impact — between what governments and institutions announce and what their citizens and stakeholders actually experience.

We provide the intellectual frameworks, human capital development, and regulatory thinking that converts well-intentioned mandates into measurable outcomes. Our work is primarily delivered through offline engagements: training programmes, advisory sessions, and policy workshops conducted in person, with full financial transparency.

Our digital presence is not our product. It is our proof — proof that the firm is real, verifiable, and serious about its mandate. What you read here is the framework; the delivery happens in person.

Digital Economy & Leadership Experts

Kemabonta,Ibiwari Uloma is a technology attorney and consultant specializing in the digital economy for international, national, and private organizations. He is a senior member of the Nigerian tech innovation ecosystem and contributed, among other Stakeholders, to the establishment of agencies such as NITDA, NIRA, and the Local Content Program in ICT.

Key Consultancies & Global Roles

Inye has served as a World Bank Consultant for the 2019 Digital Economy Diagnostics for Africa and lead consultant for the Nigeria GovTech Community Initiative. As a Key Expert for the European Union, he supported broadband infrastructure expansion in Nigeria. Currently, he consults for the German GIZ & Impact Investment Foundation on Impact Investing, developing a National Impact Investing Policy Framework. Additionally, he serves as an Expert Trainer on Internet Governance for the African Union Commission and represents technology-enabled businesses in Africa as the Nigerian coordinator for AfICTA.

Regulatory & Policy Leadership

His policy leadership includes drafting a regulatory framework for Cyber Insurance in Nigeria for NITDA and NAICOM, as well as helping draft major legislation such as the E-government and Digital Economy Bill, CyberCrime Bill, and the NITDA Bill. He was the Pioneer National Coordinator of the Office for Nigerian Content Development (ONC) and is the current chair of the Policy and Regulation Committee for the NCDMB ICT Sector Working Group. Furthermore, he formerly headed the IT Standards and Regulation Department at NITDA.

Advocacy & Education

Inye passionately advocates for creative regulatory strategies to expand broadband access and improve digital, financial, and internet literacy. He champions local content implementation to create jobs and strengthen the naira. He also consults for state ICT agencies on using stakeholder-driven regulations to catalyze growth and serves as a Director in the Financial Inclusion Centers Professionals Network PLC.

Kemabonta, Ibiwari Uloma is a John Maxwell certified Leadership Development Consultant and Human Capital Development Coach and an IATA certified travel and tours professional and consultant.

Apart from managing her tour company, she runs aTIP (Africa Travel Information Platform) a platform purely dedicated to the development and promotion of sustainable tourism in Africa.

She is currently the national chairperson for the NANTA Capacity Development and Accreditation Committee and has served the organization as the Deputy Vice President in Abuja.
